The Livepeer ecosystem has spent the past year pursuing the Cascade vision — transitioning from pure streaming infrastructure to a platform capable of powering real-time AI video compute. Recent data validates this direction: network fees have tripled year-over-year, with AI inference now driving over 72% of fees. This momentum reveals the need for a more refined product vision.

## The Expanding Video Market

Video is evolving beyond traditional streaming. AI now generates and enhances entire worlds and scenes in real-time. Three emerging use cases define this shift:

- **Gaming:** AI generates video and interactive worlds in real-time
- **Analysis:** Video streams are analyzed programmatically for instant insights
- **Avatar Enhancement:** Real-time style transfer enables agents to participate economically

Video world models and real-time AI video are converging — both require frame-by-frame AI output with low latency. This demands tremendous GPU compute alongside specialized low-latency video infrastructure, where Livepeer has unique advantages.

## The Core Opportunity

Livepeer should become the leading AI infrastructure for real-time video. From interactive streaming to generative world models, its decentralized GPU network offers optimal compute for cutting-edge AI video workflows.

### Required Capabilities

1. **Workflow Deployment:** Users deploy real-time AI workflows and request inference
2. **Industry-Leading Latency:** Competitive latency for real-time AI workflows
3. **Cost-Effective Scalability:** Pay-as-you-go pricing enabling dynamic capacity adjustment

## Three Strategic Value Propositions

### 1. Low-Latency Infrastructure for Real-Time AI

Support custom workflow deployment beyond base model inference. Users chain models together with pre/post-processors. Livepeer's specialized video stacks provide advantages over generic GPU clouds.

### 2. Cost-Effective Scalability

Livepeer's open marketplace enables on-demand, pay-per-use compute at scale, potentially delivering the 10x+ cost reductions already demonstrated in transcoding.

### 3. Community-Driven, Open-Source Access

Open-source stack and token-based ownership enable user control and community governance.

## Network Evolution Roadmap

Four development milestones define the path forward:

1. **Network MVP:** Establish performance SLAs, measure latency benchmarks, enhance low-latency clients
2. **Network as Product:** Deliver against SLAs; implement selection algorithms, failover, and competitive pricing
3. **Extensibility:** Enable workflow deployment and resource provisioning with updated LPT incentives
4. **Parallel Scalability:** Manage resource clusters for parallel workflow execution beyond single-GPU inference

## Market Implications

Target users are startups building vertical-specific real-time AI platforms. The ecosystem should strategically incentivize multiple companies per category — gaming, robotics, synthetic data, monitoring, analysis.

As platforms scale, some may graduate to building directly on the network, similar to companies graduating from Heroku to AWS.

Real-time AI compute demands approximately 1,000x the resources of text or image AI. With Livepeer already processing millions of real-time AI inference minutes weekly, the ecosystem is positioned to capture this opportunity.
