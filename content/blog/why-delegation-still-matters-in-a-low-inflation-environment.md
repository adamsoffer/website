As Livepeer's token inflation rate adjusts dynamically in response to network participation, inflation has begun declining in certain rounds. With staking participation near 50%, a natural question arises: if inflation-based rewards are shrinking, does delegation still matter? The answer is yes — more than ever.

## Inflation Is Starting to Decline, But Rewards Aren't Gone

Livepeer uses a dynamic inflation model:

- When participation falls below 50%, inflation increases to encourage staking
- When participation exceeds 50%, inflation decreases, minimizing token dilution

Currently hovering around 50% participation, inflation is beginning to decline — though approximately 30,000 LPT tokens are still minted daily.

As the protocol matures, emphasis shifts toward usage-based fees from video and AI compute services. Orchestrators earn fees directly, and delegators receive shares if their chosen orchestrator distributes them.

## Delegation Powers the Network

### Delegation Helps Secure and Stabilize the Network

Livepeer depends on orchestrators — node operators transcoding video and executing inference workloads. Delegating LPT:

- Signals trust toward specific node operators
- Enables operators to secure additional network work
- Strengthens distributed responsibility and performance

Where enabled, slashing penalizes orchestrators and delegators for malicious behavior, so delegating toward high-performing nodes helps maintain network integrity.

### Delegation Drives Economic Alignment

Delegation is an economic relationship. Delegators direct capital toward work and fees.

Operators delivering superior performance attract greater stake and more assignments, creating incentive alignment:

- Delegators earn reward portions
- Operators benefit from uptime and quality
- The network directs work toward the most efficient providers

## More Usage = More Rewards

Recent months have shown substantial growth in usage-based network revenue. Network fees have peaked, predominantly from AI-powered video compute and real-time streaming.

This matters:

- Node operators earn more from legitimate work
- Delegators receive portions of real economic activity
- The system depends less on token inflation

Emerging verticals — AI video, decentralized streaming, Farcaster applications — are expected to drive increasing demand.

## Why Delegation Matters

- **Network Security:** Delegation maintains decentralization and resilience
- **Economic Efficiency:** Routes work toward high-performing operators
- **Aligned Incentives:** Delegators earn from both emissions and usage-based fees
- **Exposure to Growth:** Usage expansion connects delegators to rising demand
- **Governance Power:** Delegated tokens contribute toward onchain governance
