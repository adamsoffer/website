Welcome to Livepeer Onchain Builders, a content series spotlighting the Special Purpose Entities (SPEs) funded by the Livepeer onchain treasury. These initiatives drive protocol usage, expand infrastructure, and push the boundaries of decentralized video and AI.

First up: Streamplace — an open-source video streaming platform designed to power decentralized social applications with real-time, creator-first infrastructure. It aims to make livestreaming and video hosting as seamless as mainstream platforms, but built on open protocols and self-sovereign identity.

## What is an SPE?

A Special Purpose Entity (SPE) is a focused, community-funded team contributing to the Livepeer ecosystem. SPEs operate independently to build infrastructure, applications, or tooling that expand the protocol.

SPEs matter because:

- They decentralize development across the ecosystem
- They fund public goods initiatives
- They allow communities to direct resources strategically
- They drive protocol usage, increasing fees and rewards for delegators

## From Aquareum to Streamplace

Streamplace evolved from Aquareum, maintaining the same team and mission while sharpening focus: building foundational video infrastructure for decentralized social platforms like Farcaster and AT Protocol.

Previous deliverables:

- Unified node bundling Livepeer stack with indexing and playback
- App releases across iOS, Android, and Web
- Native integrations with AT Protocol and Farcaster
- C2PA metadata and content provenance support

The current phase is funded with 100,000 LPT treasury support.

## Why Streamplace Matters

Video is the heart of online social interaction. Streamplace addresses infrastructure gaps by:

- **Transcoding every livestream through Livepeer**, enabling decentralized, cost-effective global delivery
- **Powering partner platforms** like Skylight Social, a TikTok alternative backed by Mark Cuban that reached #1 entertainment status on the App Store
- **Simplifying deployment** through single-binary nodes anyone can operate
- **Championing public goods** with 100% open-source code
